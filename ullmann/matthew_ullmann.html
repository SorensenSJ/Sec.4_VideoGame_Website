Uncharted 4 Review

Uncharted 4

Rating 9/10

This game deserves a 9 out of 10 because it contains a great story along with amazing gameplay. What really blows this game out of thr water is its stunning visuals. Things that make the game even better is its amusing multiplayer. What dosen't make this a perfect 10/10 is its bloated third act which is when the content becomes a little repetitive from its previous 3 games.

Review

Uncharted 4: A Theif's End is a remarkable acheivement in blockbuster storytelling and graphical beauty. Though it's let down by a lack of immmagination and some self-indulgence, especially in a third act that drags on far too long. Uncharted 4 carries on the series' proud tradition of peerless polish and style, wiht a great multiplayer component to boot.Most importantly, it's a gentle sendoff to the rag-tag group of characters we've known for nine years. A worthy thief's end, indeed. (IGN The Verdict)

History of the Uncharted Series

In Uncharted: Drake’s Fortune, the first in the series, the protagonist Nathan Drake, supposed descendant of Sir Francis Drake, goes on the hunt to find the lost treasure of El Dorado in South America, with the help of his mentor Sully and girlfriend Elena Fisher. They become wanted by pirates, led by Eddy Raja and a group of mercenaries led by Atoq Navarro. However, staring into the treasure that Drake desires, it can make a human possessed by the demons/monster of the cursed treasure.

In Uncharted 2: Among Thieves, Nathan takes a journey across the Nepal and the Himalayas in the search of a deserted city, called Shambhala. Uncharted 2 consists of the same 3 people, but also a brand new character, Chloe Frazer, a new love interest who has had a history with Nathan. The second game also introduces a multiplayer mode to the series.

Uncharted 3: Drake’s Deception was released in November of 2011, on the Playstation 3 console. The story of this game focuses on Nate and Sully on a mission to a legendary lost city. This lost city will ultimately take him to the Arabian Peninsula and the vast wasteland of the huge Rub’ al Khali desert, in the search for “Iram of the Pillars”. This game provides the players with multiple perspectives, which means you can play as other players, not just Nate.
